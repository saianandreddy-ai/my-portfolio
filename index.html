About Me

I didn't start with AI. I grew into it.

My journey began writing backend code in Hyderabad, debugging APIs late into the night, learning how systems talk to each other. Back then, intelligence in software meant a well optimized SQL query. I had no idea that a decade later I would be building systems that think.

Over 10 years, I moved through every layer of the stack. From legacy Python migrations to real time Kafka pipelines, from fraud detection models to enterprise LLM copilots serving thousands of users. Each role taught me something the previous one couldn't. Healthcare taught me precision. Banking taught me trust. Retail taught me scale. Insurance taught me consequence.

Today I work at the intersection of language models and real world decisions. I build RAG pipelines that help underwriters make faster calls. I design GenAI systems that process claims documents in seconds instead of hours. I architect retrieval systems that don't just find information but understand context.

What drives me isn't the technology itself. It's the moment a workflow that used to take a team three days gets done in three minutes. That's the version of AI I'm interested in. Not the demo, but the deployment.

I'm based in New York, currently at New York Life Insurance, and I'm always thinking about what comes next in the GenAI space. If you're building something ambitious, I'd love to hear about it.
